Privacy Policy

Effective Date: April 26, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Nexus Media Archiver ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, and discloses information from users ("you") when you use the Nexus application.

1. Information Access and Collection

Nexus is designed to be a secure media archival tool. To provide its core features, our application requests access to specific categories of data through Google OAuth 2.0:

  • Google Identity Information: We access your unique Google ID (`sub`) and email address to authenticate your session and derivation of your private encryption keys locally on your device.
  • YouTube Data: We access your YouTube account to upload your designated media content to private playlists and to manage these archives for recovery purposes.
  • Google Drive Files: We access your Google Drive to store and synchronize a small metadata index file (`nexus.db`) that keeps track of your media archives.

2. Use of Google User Data

We use the information received from Google APIs strictly to provide and improve the functionality of the Nexus Media Archiver. Specifically:

  • Archival Management: Encrypted media is packaged and uploaded to your private cloud playlists for backup.
  • Metadata Synchronization: The metadata index is synced via your Google Drive to allow you to see your archive tree across your authorized devices.

We do not use your Google user data for any other purpose, such as advertising, marketing, or profiling.

Google API Limited Use Disclosure:
Nexus Media Archiver's use and transfer to any other app of information received from Google APIs will adhere to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

3. Data Storage and Protection

Security Mechanisms: All user content is protected by high-grade XChaCha20-Poly1305 (256-bit) authenticated encryption. This encryption happens locally on your device before the data is transmitted to any cloud service. We have a zero-knowledge architecture: we do not possess the keys to decrypt your information.

Storage: Your data is stored within your own Google accounts (YouTube and Google Drive). We do not host your data on our own servers.

4. Data Retention and Deletion

Retention: We do not personally retain your Google user data. Your archives remain in your Google account as long as you choose to keep them there.

Deletion: You have full control over your data. You can delete your media archives at any time by removing the playlists or videos from your YouTube account and deleting the `nexus.db` file from your Google Drive. If you uninstall Nexus, your cloud-stored data remains untouched until you manually delete it through Google's interface.

5. Information Sharing and Disclosure

No Sharing: Nexus does not share, transfer, or disclose Google user data to any third-party entities, with the following very narrow exceptions:

  • To comply with valid legal processes.
  • Where specifically required to provide or improve the core features of the application.

No Sale of Data: We do NOT sell your Google user data to any third parties.

6. Changes to This Policy

We may update our Privacy Policy once we add new features. We recommend users review this page periodically for any changes. Use of the app constitutes acceptance of the current policy.

7. Contact Us

For support or legal inquiries, please contact us at our GitHub Repository.
